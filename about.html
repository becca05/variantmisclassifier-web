Allele-Specific Somatic Gene Mutations by Ancestry Groups

Rebecca D. Pollard*1,2, Matthew D. Wilkerson3,4

2. Metis Foundation, 300 Convent St #1330, San Antonio, TX 78205, USA

Abstract

Standard medical genetics databases primarily include disease and standard sequences for caucasian individuals. Here, we created the alleleFinder repository and searchable database to provide researchers access to somatic gene mutation's allele frequencies in African, Latino/Admixed American, Ashkenazi Jewish, East Asian, Finnish European, Estonian, North-Western European and Southern European individuals. We used publicly available data from the Catalogue of Somatic Mutations In Cancer (COSMIC v96) and Genome Aggregation Database (GnomAD) from the Linkage disequilibrium V2 to find the somatic gene mutation's allele frequencies for each ancestry. We then identified data disparities to identify deficiencies in the dataset. The alleleFinder repository and searchable database are intended to help researchers identify common population variants for somatic gene mutations and assist researchers in annotating genomic sequencing data for populations. Access to additional ancestry population-based genomic variant annotations may help accelerate the interpretation of genomic sequencing data and genotype-first efforts to trace genomic variants back to genetic disorders.
